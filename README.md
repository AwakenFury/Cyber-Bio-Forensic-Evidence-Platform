## Project Summary
Cyber Bio Forensic Evidence Platform

## Mission

Create a digital investigation system capable of organizing legal, technical, and documentary evidence into a structured case file that can be reviewed by attorneys, investigators, auditors, or a court. The system's purpose is to preserve evidence, document events, and maintain an organized record of what occurred over time. It should clearly distinguish documented facts from investigative theories or questions requiring additional evidence.

## Background

The documents span approximately fourteen years and describe multiple interconnected matters involving housing, disability accommodations, benefits administration, civil litigation, and concerns about digital security.

The common thread throughout the files is the effort to preserve records, obtain missing documentation, and organize evidence relating to interactions with agencies and housing management.

 - Why Cyber Bio Exists

Cyber Bio is no longer just a cybersecurity dashboard.

It is becoming a digital forensic workspace where every document, event, and piece of technical evidence can be organized into a single investigative record.

The project combines:

 - Digital forensics.
 - Case management.
 - Document management.
 - Evidence preservation.
 - Technical logging.
 - Timeline reconstruction.
 - Court-ready reporting.
 - Long-Term Vision

The completed Cyber Bio Forensic Evidence Platform would function as a centralized investigation environment that allows a user to:

 - Maintain multiple case files.
 - Store and categorize documentary evidence.
 - Record digital evidence with integrity metadata.
 - Build chronological timelines.
 - Track chain of custody.
 - Generate organized reports and exhibit lists.
 - Cross-reference evidence across legal, administrative, and technical matters.

Rather than focusing on proving a particular allegation, the platform's primary purpose is to ensure that evidence is preserved, organized, searchable, and presented in a structured manner so that others—such as attorneys, investigators, or courts—can independently evaluate the underlying facts.
